
## Expectations and Policies
### Student Hours
Your success in this (and any) class is your responsibility.  If you find at any time during the term that you do not understand or cannot meet the course requirements, you should speak to me immediately.

In addition, I am more than happy to discuss an assignment, brainstorm, talk over an outline, and discuss grades at any time this semester. Indeed, this is often the best part of my job. However, because email is inefficient for commenting on drafts, I strongly encourage you set up a video chat appointment if you would like feedback on early work.

### Communicating with your Professor
I am willing and happy to listen to all comments, complaints, and suggestions regarding the class. In addition, do not hesitate to let me know if you have any concerns about your work in the class or about anything that is keeping you from doing your best (this includes a range of issues from learning disabilities, illness, personal or family problems, access to basic needs such as food and housing, etc...).  I am also available to talk to about college more generally, future careers, issues in your life, etc... 
 
Finally, I am open to questions about grades.  All that I ask is that you ask me the class period following the one in which the assignment is returned.  This will give you time to look at your work more objectively. I prefer to communicate with students in person, but I will, of course, respond to emails as well.  You can expect an email reply from me within 24 hours, excluding holidays and weekends. 

### Email
To help me be more efficient, please put “ENGL 388: [your concerns/questions]” in the subject line of your emails. This will help your message stand out from the rest of the emails for the day, meaning you will be a quicker response.  I will do my best to respond to your emails as quickly as possible, but also know that I am most likely to respond in a quick manner during regular working (and/or office) hours.  Also, please keep all emails fairly formal (e.g., have an opening / greeting and a closing / salutation).  Overly informal emails reflect poorly on the sender.

Assignment Submission
To help us all stay organized and on track, all assignments will be submitted via Canvas. Please do not email me your assignments or share links from SharePoint or Google.docs unless I specifically request them. Instead, please type your responses into Canvas or, when requested, upload your project as a .docx, .pptx, .or pdf. If you’re not sure how to upload assignments, you can find directions here.  

Attendance
Attendance plays an essential role in learning; you are invited, encouraged, and expected to attend all class/lab meetings. Attendance will be important not only for your learning, but also for our ability to build a community together and maintain a sense of connection and commitment to one another during this time of imposed physical distance. Your presence in class matters. However, I recognize that there are unavoidable circumstances that sometimes make it impossible for you to attend class. If you will not be in class for any reason, please inform me via email. Remember, it is your responsibility to make up work you missed in your absence. Students with disabilities should discuss their accommodations with me early in the course to work out a plan that aligns with maintaining course expectations and learning goals.

Participation
Participation is distinct from attendance and is also an essential part of this course. In-class discussions (in-person or via Teams), on-line discussion forums, responses to brief ungraded writing assignments, etc. will be factored into your participation grade. Engaging with the (real and virtual, synchronous and asynchronous) classroom space — including by helping to create an environment where all of us can learn and think well about one another — will also be factored into participation. It is important to remember that we all have different styles of expression. If you have not been able to participate in a class discussion for any reason but want to demonstrate your active engagement, please send me an email after class with a comment or an idea you had that you would have liked to share but were not able to during class. 

Late Work
All due dates are firm and extensions will only be given in extreme circumstances.   Late work (and any missed daily quizzes or free writing) will only be accepted if you have secured an excused absence (see above) or have had it approved beforehand with me. Late assignments will be docked a full letter grade for each day late.  If an assignment is not turned in within one week of the due date, you will fail that assignment. All assignments must be completed to pass this class.

Technology
Unless otherwise noted, please bring your laptop to every class period, as posting in-class writing to Canvas is a major component of this course.  Also, please make sure your battery is charged before class, since there will almost never be enough power outlets for everyone.  I expect you already understand the basics of word processing and will readily be able to meet the formatting standards for this course.  Please contact me as other technology-related issues and/or questions arise.  Lastly, please put all cell phones on a silent setting during class time.

Plagiarism
Students often plagiarize or cheat because they don’t have enough time, because they are afraid of failure, or because they just don’t see the value in doing the work themselves. If you’re feeling this way, come talk to me! You have a lot of options before the assignment is due. Stout’s student handbook suggests that students are responsible for the honest completion and representation of their work, for the appropriate citation of sources, and for respect of others' academic endeavors. Students who violate these standards must be confronted and must accept the consequences of their actions. The disciplinary procedures can be found on the Student Academic Misconduct/Academic Discipline Procedure website.

AI Assisted Writing Guidelines
The use of generative AI tools (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grammarly, CoPilot, etc.) is permitted in this course for the following activities:  
Brainstorming and refining your ideas 
Fine-tuning your research questions 
Finding information on your topic 
Drafting an outline to organize your thoughts 
Checking grammar and style 

The use of generative AI tools is not permitted in this course for the following activities: 
Impersonating you in classroom contexts, such as by using the tool to compose discussion board prompts assigned to you or content that you put into a chat. 
Completing group work that your group has assigned to you, unless it is mutually agreed upon that you may utilize the tool.  
Writing a draft of a writing assignment. 
Writing entire sentences, paragraphs or papers to complete class assignments.  

You are responsible for the information you submit based on an AI query (for instance, that it does not violate intellectual property laws, or contain misinformation or unethical content). Your use of AI tools must be properly documented and cited in order to stay within university policies on academic honesty. 

For example, text generated using ChatGPT-3 (or another AI generator) should include a citation such as: 

“Chat-GPT-5. (YYYY, Month DD of query). “Text of your query.” Generated using OpenAI. https://chat.openai.com/” 

Material generated using other tools should follow a similar citation convention. If a tool is used in an assignment, students must also include a brief (2-3 sentences) description of how they used the tool.

Any assignment that is found to have used generative AI tools in unauthorized ways is a violation of scholastic dishonesty and will be treated as such.. When in doubt about permitted usage, use citation, or ask for clarification.

Diversity Statement
To create an inclusive classroom where everyone can do their best work, participate, and learn, it is imperative that we treat each other with respect.  This includes speaking to each other with respect, conducting discussion professionally, using correct pronouns and forms of address with all members of the class, avoiding personal attacks, not dominating the discussion, and not disrupting the class. I also see the diversity of my students (in terms of race, ethnicity, class, gender identity/expression, background, religion, etc.) as a resource for learning cooperatively and collaboratively, and I welcome civil debate and disagreement. We will discuss topics about which people feel strongly and about which there will be a variety of different perspectives on the subject.  I want to foster an environment where everyone can express their ideas. These different perspectives and views will make, I hope, for a lively and interesting class.

Grading Policy
Grades will be determined based upon the following scheme:

	A	94-100		C	74-76
	A-	90 - 93		C-	70-73
	B+	87-89		D+	67-69
	B	84-86		D	64-66
	B-	80-83 		D-	60-63
	C+	77-79		F		62 and below

The following translates the above numerical system into slightly more specific terms:

“A”:  	The student’s work is excellent and greatly exceeds course requirements and expectations.  The work is thorough, complete, coherent, supported sufficiently, and demonstrates a superior understanding of the course content.

“B”:  	The student’s work is good and exceeds the course requirements and expectations.  The work is sufficiently developed, organized, and supported, and demonstrates a solid understanding of the course content.   

“C”:  	The student’s work is average and meets all the course requirements and expectations.  The work may have clear, but underdeveloped ideas, or it does not engage the course content as well as it could.  The work includes all the necessary elements, but does not excel in any of them.

“D”:  	The student’s work is below average, but still worthy of course credit.

“F”:  	The student’s work does not meet all the course requirements and is not worthy of credit.

Scores will be made available online via Canvas but are to be considered unofficial.  Official grades are kept offline by the instructor.  Email the instructor or come to office hours if you have a question.  This course meets Domain I and Standards 1 and 6 of the Wisconsin Teaching Standards.
