Overview and Objectives

English 388 is a three-credit, hands-on course that introduces you to the process of creating and managing a content strategy for the web. Throughout the semester, you’ll work with a real client to evaluate, revise, and redesign digital content so it’s clear, consistent, and tailored to its audience. Along the way, you’ll gain practical experience in analyzing user needs, shaping online information, and collaborating on professional-level projects.

At the completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Apply a variety of contemporary rhetorical, visual, mechanical, and technical guidelines to the evaluation and creation of user-centered online artifacts; 
  • Become conversant in vocabulary relevant to audience analysis, information architecture, content strategy, interface and visual design, and usability; 
  • Develop the ability to write with clarity, concision, and accuracy in professional contexts;
  • Cultivate efficient, ethical research and writing habits; 
  • Navigate a collaborative client-based content strategy project in a professional manner.

The class will attempt to fulfill these goals by:

  • Completing required course readings and discussions;
  • Becoming familiar with a number of writing-related web technologies;
  • Developing a content strategy;
  • Completing a large-scale online writing and design project; and
  • Providing thoughtful feedback on peers’ work.
